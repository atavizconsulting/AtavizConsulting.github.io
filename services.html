Data Visualization

Want informative reports and dashboards to access and send out to your employees?

Data visualization is the most misunderstood and underutilized part of data analytics. What if you could have a weekly report emailed to your team automatically that focuses on foot traffic and closing percentage? Then you could also have a monthly report automatically emailed to you that focuses on sold units and gross profit? These aren't just possibilities, these are the steps businesses are taking every day to inform and improve those conversations with management about how to improve the bottom line.

Additionally, what if you had an interactive dashboard you could look at where you can hover over portions of a chart for more information, or filter several charts with just one click? Now, all of that is both affordable and within reach. And, you don't need to purchase any additional software! Data dashboards are the new way to visualize your KPIs while having the ability to highlight, filter, and deep dive into the data with just the click of a mouse.

Forecasting

What to expect next month? Next year?

Data science combines the techniques of data analytics with statistics and programming in order to accurately forecast and predict future events based on past and current data. We have several forecasting methods at our disposal for a variety of use cases, including:

  • basic straight-line method
  • rolling averages
  • linear regressions
  • more complex regressions (polynomial, etc.)
  • exponential smoothing (time series analysis)
